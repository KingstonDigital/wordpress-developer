Why It Matters

Technical SEO Is the Foundation

You can't build rankings on a broken site. Technical SEO isn't optional — it's what everything else depends on.

Speed Affects Rankings Directly

Google confirmed page speed as a ranking factor for both desktop and mobile. Slow sites get ranked lower and have higher bounce rates. A 1-second improvement in load time can meaningfully increase conversions for Toronto businesses.

Crawl Budget Is Finite

Google allocates a limited crawl budget to each site. If it's wasted on duplicate pages, blocked resources, or low-value URLs, your important pages get crawled less frequently — slowing how quickly new content ranks.

Architecture Determines Authority

Google distributes link equity through your site based on its structure. A properly siloed architecture with clean pillar and cluster pages concentrates authority where it counts — on the pages you most want to rank. A flat, unorganised site spreads that authority thin across everything equally.

GSC Issues Go Unnoticed for Months

Most business owners don't check Google Search Console regularly. Index coverage errors, manual actions, and crawl anomalies can silently suppress rankings for months. We monitor it so you don't have to.

Technical SEO FAQs

How do I know if my site has technical SEO issues? +
Most sites do — even well-built ones accumulate technical debt over time through plugin updates, content changes, and platform migrations. Signs include slow load times, pages not showing up in Google, a sudden drop in organic traffic, or a Google Search Console account showing crawl errors. The only reliable way to know is a proper technical audit.
What's included in a technical SEO audit? +
Our technical audit covers Core Web Vitals and page speed, crawlability and robots.txt configuration, XML sitemap health, index coverage via Google Search Console, internal linking structure, duplicate content and canonical tags, mobile usability, HTTPS and security signals, schema markup validation, and redirect chains. You get a prioritized report with every issue ranked by impact.
What does site architecture have to do with SEO? +
Site architecture determines how Google distributes authority across your pages. A well-structured site with clear pillar and cluster pages, clean internal linking, and no duplicate content issues helps Google understand what you do and rank your most important pages higher. Poorly structured sites — with orphaned pages, broken links, and no clear hierarchy — dilute that authority and make it harder to rank for anything competitively.
Do you work on WordPress sites specifically? +
Yes — WordPress is our primary platform and we understand its technical quirks inside out. From plugin conflicts and bloated databases to theme-generated render-blocking CSS and server configuration, we know where WordPress sites typically go wrong and how to fix them efficiently.
How long does a technical SEO fix take? +
Quick wins like fixing crawl errors, submitting corrected sitemaps, and adding missing schema can be done within the first week. Deeper improvements like Core Web Vitals optimization and site architecture restructuring typically take 2–4 weeks. You'll see a prioritized roadmap in your audit report so you know exactly what to expect and when.
